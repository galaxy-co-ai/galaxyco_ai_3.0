# Investor Pre-Meeting Prep
## Quick Sync Before the Team Demo

---

## PURPOSE OF THIS MEETING

The investor set up the 1pm meeting with engineers and designers. This pre-meeting is likely to:
1. Align on goals for the 1pm demo
2. Discuss what the investor needs to see
3. Possibly finalize investment terms

---

## KEY POINTS TO COVER

### 1. Confirm Demo Strategy
> "For the team demo, I'm focusing on recruitment - showing them the technical depth and interesting problems. Does that align with what you're hoping they see?"

### 2. Clarify Investment Timeline
> "Assuming the team demo goes well, what's the timeline for finalizing the seed round?"

### 3. Understand Their Lens
> "What do you think will most impress the engineers and designers you've invited? Any specific concerns I should address?"

---

## WHAT TO SHOW (if they want to see the product)

Keep this brief - save the full demo for 1pm:
1. Neptune doing one multi-step action (2 min max)
2. Quick dashboard overview (1 min)
3. Mention the stats: 130K lines, 259 endpoints, production-ready

---

## QUESTIONS TO ASK

1. "Who exactly is coming at 1pm? What are their backgrounds?"
2. "Are they evaluating this as potential employees, or just advising?"
3. "What's the one thing you most want them to walk away thinking?"
4. "Is there anything about the product or pitch I should avoid?"

---

## TERMS TO BE READY TO DISCUSS

If investment terms come up:
- Seed round: $500K minimum
- Valuation: Have your number ready (typically 2-4x the raise for seed)
- Use of funds: First hires (engineering + design), runway for 12-18 months
- Milestones: Beta launch, first paying customers, team of 3-5

---

## YOUR 30-SECOND PITCH (if needed)

> "GalaxyCo.ai is an AI-native business operating system. Instead of juggling Salesforce, HubSpot, QuickBooks, and Notion, you get one unified platform with Neptune - an AI assistant that actually executes, not just chats.
>
> We're at 90% feature complete with 130K lines of production code. The tech is solid. Now I need the founding team to take it to market. That's what today is about."

---

## BODY LANGUAGE NOTES

- Investor meetings should feel like a partnership conversation, not a pitch
- Ask as many questions as you answer
- Be confident but not defensive
- It's okay to say "I don't know, but here's how I'd find out"

---

## AFTER THIS MEETING

- 30 minutes to review demo data
- 15 minutes to test Neptune
- 10 minutes to review cheat sheet
- 5 minutes before 1pm: water, bathroom, deep breath

---

*This investor already believes in you. This meeting is coordination, not persuasion.*
